All suppliers of goods or services create GST invoices on a daily basis, but hopefully hey also get paid by their clients. This means that when you receive a payment for one of your invoices, you should issue a receipt voucher.


What is a payment voucher?

A receipt, also known as payment voucher, is a document which attests that a payment has been made to confirm a supply of goods / services. In the case of goods, a receipt serves the purpose of proof of ownership. A receipt is a legally binding commercial document that is used to prove that goods or services of a certain value have been received and paid for.


Invoice vs Receipt (Payment Voucher)

During a sale, a supplier and a buyer enter into a business arrangement that usually includes a written agreement outlining the transaction terms. These agreements typically involve invoices and receipts.

An invoice, also called a bill, is used in accounting to record a sale transaction, whereas a receipt is used to account for payments. Simply put, an invoice is issued before payment, while a receipt is issued after payment.


GST Advance Payment Receipt

When a customer makes an advance payment, tax liability arises before issuing an invoice and supplying goods or services. This payment must be recorded in accounting, as the bank has already registered the payment date.

If the tax is not paid separately by the customer at the time of payment, the advance payment is considered inclusive of tax. As per GST rules, the supplier must issue an advance receipt voucher to the customer on the date of payment receipt. This voucher should have a unique serial number for the financial year.


Receipt Voucher Format

As per clause (d) in subsection (3) of section 31 of the GST Act, a receipt voucher must contain:

  • Name, address, and GSTIN of the supplier
  • Unique serial number for the financial year
  • Date of issue
  • Name, address, and GSTIN (or UIN if unregistered) of the buyer
  • Description of goods or services
  • Advance payment amount
  • Tax rate (CGST, SGST/IGST/UTGST breakdown)
  • Place of supply with state name and code
  • Reverse charge applicability
  • Signature of the supplier or authorized representative

Payment Report - Statement of Accounts

A statement of accounts is a detailed report of transactions related to a specific client. It includes both billings and payments over a specific period, concluding with the ending balance. This helps customers track pending sales and payments.

A statement of accounts usually includes:

  • Total of unpaid invoices
  • Invoice number, date, and total amount for each issued invoice
  • Credit note details (number, date, and amount issued)
  • Payment dates and total amounts received
  • Net remaining balance of all transactions
